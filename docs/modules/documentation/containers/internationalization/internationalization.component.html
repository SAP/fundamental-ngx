Directionality *
There are languages written in left-to-right(LTR - or sinistrodextrally) or in right-to-left(RTL or dextrosinistrally). Writing direction is left to right in most European languages, right-to-left in Hebrew and Arabic, or both in boustrophedon scripts, and optionally vertical in some Asian languages. In Hebrew and Arabic, the main direction of reading flows from right to left and the starting point of the screen is in the top right corner. The idea is the localized forms must comply to the requirements applicable for the intended language and location.
